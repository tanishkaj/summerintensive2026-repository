About Me

I'm a second-year Biology undergraduate at NYU, minoring in Global Public Health and a Presidential Honors Scholar. I'm broadly interested in how molecular mechanisms drive cell survival — particularly in contexts where that survival has clinical consequences.

My interest in cellular quiescence started with reading about Candida albicans: quiescent fungal cells survive antifungal treatment and can re-enter the cell cycle later, which may contribute to persistent and drug-resistant infections. The Gresham Lab uses S. cerevisiae as a model to understand exactly how cells regulate entry into and exit from this state — and I find that question genuinely fascinating.

What's Next

Coming Fall 2026

Sic1 & Cellular Quiescence

Continuing in the Gresham Lab this fall, I'll be investigating whether Sic1 — a cyclin-dependent kinase inhibitor that blocks cell-cycle entry — accumulates at higher levels in quiescent cells compared to actively proliferating ones. If so, Sic1 abundance could serve as a molecular marker of quiescence. The project uses iodixanol density-gradient centrifugation to separate quiescent and proliferating yeast populations, followed by Western blotting to quantify Sic1 protein levels.
